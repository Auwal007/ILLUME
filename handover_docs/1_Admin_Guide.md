# ILLUME Bespoke Couture — Admin & Content Management Guide

Welcome to your website's Administration Portal! This guide is designed to help you, your store managers, and design team manage the content of **illume.ng** without writing a single line of code.

---

## 1. Accessing the Admin Panel

To log in to your administration panel:

1.  Open your web browser and go to: **[https://illume.ng/admin](https://illume.ng/admin)**
2.  Enter the login credentials:
    *   **Email Address:** `admin@illume.ng`
    *   **Password:** `admin123`
3.  Click the **Sign In** button.

> [!WARNING]
> **Change Your Password:** Once you log in for the first time, please ask us to change your password or update it in the database for security reasons. Do not share these credentials over insecure chats.

---

## 2. Navigating the Dashboard Overview
When you first log in, you will be taken to the **Dashboard Overview**. Here, you will see a summary of your site's activities:
*   **Total Inquiries:** The number of potential clients who have filled out the contact form.
*   **Products:** The total number of dresses, suits, and outfits currently registered in your catalogue.
*   **Categories:** The main departments (e.g., Bridals, Suiting) that divide your catalogue.
*   **Testimonials:** The customer reviews active on your homepage.
*   **Recent Inquiries Table:** A quick list of the latest 5 inquiries showing client names, emails, requested services, and follow-up status.

To navigate between sections, use the **Black Sidebar** on the left side of your screen (or the toggle menu on mobile).

---

## 3. Managing Your Products (The Catalogue)
Your products are the bespoke garments displayed under **Explore Collections** and **Catalogue**.

### How to Add a New Garment:
1.  Click on **Products** in the sidebar.
2.  Click the **Add New Product** button.
3.  Fill in the required details:
    *   **Product Name:** E.g., *Sienna Gold Silk Gown*.
    *   **Slug:** This is the web address suffix (e.g., `sienna-gold-silk-gown`). The system usually fills this out automatically based on the name.
    *   **Price (Optional):** Enter the price in Naira (e.g., `550000`). If it is a custom piece that requires a consultation to quote, you can leave it blank.
    *   **Category:** Select where it belongs (e.g., *Evening Couture*).
    *   **Description:** Write a brief luxury description highlighting fabrics, cut, and beadwork details.
    *   **Product Images:** Provide the URL or path to the product image.
    *   **Featured Masterpiece:** Tick this box if you want this specific garment to be highlighted on the Homepage slider.
4.  Click **Save Product**.

### How to Edit or Delete a Product:
*   On the **Products** page, locate the garment in the list.
*   Click **Edit** to modify descriptions, adjust prices, or change images.
*   Click **Delete** (in red) to permanently remove the item from the site.

## 4. Managing Categories and Collections

What is the difference?
*   **Categories** are functional groupings (e.g., *Bridals & Asoebi*, *Suits & Dinner*). Every product belongs to one category.
*   **Collections** are seasonal campaigns or design books (e.g., *Rain / Resplendence 2026*, *Heritage Collection*).

### Managing Categories:
1.  Click on **Categories** in the sidebar.
2.  Here you can see the active list (*Bridals & Asoebi*, *Suits & Dinner*, *African Luxury*, *Evening Couture*).
3.  You can add a new category by entering a Name and Description, or delete an existing category.

### Managing Collections:
1.  Click on **Collections** in the sidebar.
2.  You can create a collection, assign a banner image, and write an editorial description.
3.  To add products to a collection, simply edit the product and select the collection it belongs to.

---

## 5. Reviewing Client Inquiries (Leads)
When clients fill out the contact form on your site, their details are stored in your database so you never lose a lead.

1.  Click on **Inquiries** in the sidebar.
2.  You will see a list of client submissions:
    *   **Name, Email, and Phone Number**
    *   **Service Requested:** (e.g., Bespoke Gown, Bridal Styling)
    *   **Message:** Their specific requests or fitting dates.
    *   **Status Badge:**
        *   `NEW` (Blue): Unread or not yet contacted.
        *   `CONTACTED` (Yellow): You have reached out to them via phone/email/WhatsApp.
        *   `COMPLETED` (Green): The order or consultation is completed.
3.  **To change status:** Click on the dropdown menu next to their status and choose the new status. This helps your team stay coordinated.

---

## 6. Managing Homepage Testimonials
Word-of-mouth is crucial for luxury fashion. You can display reviews from your bespoke brides and suits clients.

1.  Click on **Testimonials** in the sidebar.
2.  Click **Add New Testimonial**.
3.  Fill in:
    *   **Client Name:** (e.g., *Dr. Chioma N.*)
    *   **Role/Title:** (e.g., *Bespoke Bride* or *Corporate Director*)
    *   **Rating:** Number of stars (1 to 5).
    *   **Review Content:** Paste their kind words about your fit, fabrics, and customer service.
4.  Click **Save**. The review will instantly appear on the Homepage testimonial slider.
